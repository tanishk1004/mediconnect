The Problem

Rural Healthcare is Broken

Millions of people in rural and underserved areas face life-threatening barriers to basic medical care every single day.

Distance Barrier

Rural patients travel an average of 60+ km to reach the nearest hospital, often on unpaved roads with no transport.

60+ km average travel

Unaffordable Care

Travel costs, consultation fees, and lost wages make healthcare financially impossible for low-income rural families.

70% can't afford care

Doctor Shortage

Only 1 doctor per 10,000 people in rural areas vs 1 per 300 in cities. Specialists are virtually nonexistent.

1:10,000 doctor ratio

No Health Records

Patients have no medical history tracking. Every visit starts from scratch, leading to misdiagnosis and repeated tests.

0% digital records

Maternal Mortality

Rural maternal mortality is 3x higher than urban areas due to lack of prenatal care and emergency obstetric services.

3x higher mortality

Late Diagnosis

Diseases like diabetes, hypertension, and cancer are diagnosed at advanced stages because of no routine screening.

80% late-stage diagnosis
